Preparation

No paint will perform correctly unless the proper steps are taken to prepare the surface before application. This process is called prep. Interior-painting prep is a little more involved then its exterior counterpart due to the fact that interior surfaces are generally much smoother then exterior ones, thus a larger set of imperfections in the surface must be dealt with to avoid them becoming accentuated by a fresh coat of paint.

  1. All surfaces are cleaned
  2. All loose paint is scraped and removed.
  3. All dents and nailholes are spacked
  4. All rough surfaces are sanded.
  5. All cracks are caulked.
  6. All dust is vacuumed.
  7. All surfaces requiring primer are primed.
